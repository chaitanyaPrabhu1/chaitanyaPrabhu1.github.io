About the Course

Software System Development is a graduate-level course that equips students with a comprehensive understanding of the principles, tools, and practices involved in designing and building modern software systems. Emphasizing a systems-oriented perspective, the course explores foundational and contemporary topics including Operating Systems, Computer Networks, Software Engineering methodologies, and Database Systems (both relational and NoSQL).


Students will gain practical experience with industry-relevant tools and technologies such as Bash scripting, SQL, NoSQL databases, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, front-end frameworks, RESTful APIs, Python, and Flask. The course focuses on building scalable, maintainable, and secure software systems while fostering an appreciation for system integration, version control, development workflows, and deployment strategies.


Designed for students with a fundamental background in programming, this course bridges theory and practice, preparing them to contribute effectively to complex software development projects in both research and industry settings.

Course Policy

  • Teaching Methodology

    This course is designed to help learners get the essence of principles behind good software system development. Learners will work on real-life problems to promote deeper engagement and understanding.

  • Academic Honesty

    If A helps B in a Task X → B doesn't get opportunity to do task X, B doesn't learn the skill. You may think it is okay to do it only once, but it gets wired into the brain as being "okay". If you want to help, help to learn!

  • Zero Tolerance on Plagiarism

    Any forms of cheating or plagiarism will result in a score of "0" for projects, assignments or exams. Egregious cases will result in a grade of "F" for the course.

  • Attendance Policy

    You are expected to attend all lectures and lab sessions. If you miss any quiz/activity, you will receive a '0'. There will be NO make up classes/quizzes/activities.

  • Miscellaneous Instructions

    Given the subject is based on building software systems, ensure you have a laptop with decent configuration. Please participate in in-class discussions and interact with the instructor and TAs.

  • Use of AI Tools

    Students are free to use AI tools like ChatGPT, Co-pilot, etc. However, please mention what tools and how they have been used. Remember: these tools can mislead. It's important to know the concept before using the tools.

  • Submission Deadlines

    There will be NO extensions for any graded task. Please do not wait until last minute to submit. Work with academic office for exemption if needed due to health issues/conferences/personal matters.

  • Grading Policy

    Students may request re-evaluation within a week after grade posting. No makeup activity/lab unless prior permission from Academic Office. Late submissions require medical documentation through academic office.
